## Target Markets

### Primary
1. **Non-technical users** who want to build without coding
2. **Small teams** needing full-stack tools without complexity
3. **Startups** wanting rapid prototyping

### Secondary
1. **Freelancers** needing quick project setup
2. **Educators** teaching programming basics
3. **Enterprise** teams wanting standardized dev environment

---

## Differentiation Strategy

### Short Term (0-6 months)
- Focus on **Simple Mode** as unique differentiator
- Market to non-technical users
- Build template library

### Long Term (6-18 months)
- Add **autocomplete** (compete with Copilot)
- Mobile app for monitoring
- Cloud sync for settings

---

## Competitive Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| **Copilot is better at autocomplete** | Focus on non-autocomplete features |
| **Cursor has better UX** | Simplify Simple Mode further |
| **Anthropic/LLM dependency** | Support multiple providers (Ollama, OpenRouter) |

---

## Conclusion

**Quantum Workspace fills a gap** in the market: an AI IDE for everyone — not just developers. While competitors focus on power users, Quantum serves beginners with Simple Mode while offering pro features for developers.

**Go-to-market:** "The AI IDE that anyone can use — from beginners building their first website to developers shipping to production."
